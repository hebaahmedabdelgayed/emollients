üìö Core Knowledge

Moisturizers repair the skin barrier through three key mechanisms:

  • Occlusion: Reduces TEWL by 99% with petrolatum
  • Humectancy: Glycerin increases skin hydration by 50%
  • Lipid Replacement: Ceramide mixtures restore barrier integrity
TEWL Transepidermal Water Loss: The amount of water that passively evaporates through the skin
Type Star Ingredients Mechanism Ideal For
Occlusive Agents Petrolatum, Dimethicone Forms protective film (reduces TEWL by 99%) Severe dryness, winter use
Emollients Cetyl Alcohol, Shea Butter Fills SC gaps (improves texture by 40%) Rough skin, keratosis
Humectants Glycerin, Hyaluronic Acid Attracts water (holds 1000x weight in water) Dehydrated skin, anti-aging
Physiological Lipids Ceramides (3:1:1 ratio) Mimics natural SC lipids Atopic dermatitis, barrier repair

Atopic Dermatitis

Recommendations:

  • Ceramide-dominant formulations (3:1:1 ratio)
  • Urea 5-10% for mild cases
  • Avoid fragrances and preservatives
NMF Natural Moisturizing Factors: Amino acids, PCA, lactate that maintain hydration

Psoriasis

Key strategies:

  • Keratolytics: Urea 20% + Salicylic Acid 2%
  • Pre-UV treatment with mineral oil
  • Barrier repair with cholesterol esters
